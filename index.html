A computer can be defined as a programmable, electronic device that accepts data, performs operations on that data, presents the results, and can store the data or results as needed. There are four primary operations of a computer: input, output, processing and storage. Modern computers are also frequently used for communication. The computer is a kind of information system. Information systems have six parts: people, procedures, data, software, hardware and networks. The most important part of the information system is people. Procedures are the rules which people and computers follow to meet company policy or laws.

System software enables applications to interact with computer hardware.
